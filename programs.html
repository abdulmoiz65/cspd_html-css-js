Overview of Income Tax Law
Date: December 12, 2025 | 1 Day | 6 Hours | 9:00 am to 3:00 pm
Course Overview

This course provides a structured introduction to the fundamental concepts and framework of income taxation in Pakistan under the Income Tax Ordinance, 2001. It covers the scope and scheme of the Ordinance, classification of persons, tax regimes, computation of taxable income, and the essential mechanics of tax allowances, credits, advance tax, and refunds. Emphasis is placed on understanding how tax laws apply to different types of income and taxpayers, enabling participants to interpret and apply statutory provisions with confidence. The course blends conceptual understanding with practical insight into Pakistan’s income tax system, preparing learners for both academic and professional applications.

Course Outline
  • Introduction
  • Concept of Person
  • Charge of Income Tax
  • Allowances & Credits
  • Advances & Refunds
Learning Outcomes

Participants will be able to:

  • Understand scope of income tax.
  • Identify persons as per Income Tax Ordinance, 2001.
  • Differentiate between resident and non-resident.
  • Understand various tax regimes provided under the Ordinance.
  • Understand how income tax is charged on various types of income.
  • Differentiate between allowances and credits.
  • Understand applicability of advance tax and creation of income tax refund.
Who Should Attend
  • Undergraduate and graduate students in Accounting, Finance, Economics, Business Administration, or Law seeking a foundational understanding of Pakistan’s income tax system.
  • Entry-level finance, audit, and tax professionals who need a working knowledge of the Income Tax Ordinance, 2001 for practical application in their roles.
  • Business owners and entrepreneurs who wish to understand how income tax applies to individuals and entities operating in Pakistan.
  • Candidates preparing for professional qualifications such as CA, ACCA, ICMA, or tax diplomas, where taxation forms a core component.
  • Public sector employees or trainees involved in financial regulation, compliance, or tax administration who require clarity on income tax fundamentals.

Program Overview:

Procurement is called “source-to-settle” or “procure to pay” process. The process involves a careful evaluation, selection, and creation of formal documents as well as managing the company's ongoing supplier relationships. Procurement is a complex discipline spanning many interrelated activities of a supply chain.
Procurement supports operational requirements, manages supplier base effectively & efficiently and to develop strong relationships with external and internal sources of the company. It eventually supports organizational goals and objectives.
The objective of this course is help understand the procurement management under the umbrella of Supply chain management. The course will cover the major aspects of procurement management including P2P cycle, planning, spend management, contract management & negotiation.

Topics Covered:
  • Unit 1: Supply Chain Management
  • Unit 2: Understanding Procurement
  • Unit 3: Procure to Pay Cycle
  • Unit 4: Strategic Procurement Planning
  • Unit 5: Spend Management & Commodity Strategy
  • Unit 6: Buyer – Supplier Relationship
  • Unit 7: Contract Management
  • Unit 8: Negotiation Management
Agenda: (Time wise allocation of Topics)
  • Training is designed as online medium for total 10 hours divided into 4 equal sessions 2.5 hours each. Time wise allocations of topics will be as under:-
    • Unit 1 & 2 – 2.5 hours
    • Unit 3 & 4 – 2.5 hours
    • Unit 5 & 6 – 2.5 hours
    • Unit 7 & 8 – 2.5 hours
  • All participants will be required to provide their insights and inputs on all topics, where needed
  • Exercises and case studies will be added to give practical experience to teams
Who should attend? (Target Audience)
  • Supply Chain & Procurement professionals.
  • Officers, Team Leaders, Managers & Individual contributors from any other functions of supply chain.
How will you (participant) benefit?

Will be able to:

  • Understand the essence of Procurement in competitive environment of Supply Chain
  • Understand “procure to pay cycle” & equipped with techniques to efficiently manage the same.
  • Comprehend Supply chain Strategy and its effect on Procurement policy of any given organization.
  • Apply tools & techniques to procurement inventories e.g. ABC Analysis, Spend Analysis, Commodity Strategy.
  • Draft a valid contract binding suppliers to ensure smooth deliveries of supplies or services.
  • Understand & apply negotiation techniques to arrive at a win–win situation for both parties (Buyer & supplier)

Personality Development and Work Ethics
December 11, 2025 | 1 Day | 8 Hours | 9:00 am to 5:00 pm
Course Overview

This one day training program focuses on helping participants understand the role of personal development and ethical behavior in achieving professional excellence. The training blends self-awareness, attitude building, interpersonal effectiveness, and professional ethics to create a well-rounded, confident, and responsible workforce. Through interactive discussions, personality assessments, and real-life workplace examples, participants will learn how to project a positive professional image, practice integrity, and align personal values with organizational culture.

Course Outline
  • Personality Awareness and Self-Development (3 Hours)
    • Understanding Personality and Professional Image
    • Building a Positive Mindset and Attitude
    • Emotional Intelligence and Interpersonal Skills
  • Work Ethics and Professional Conduct
    • Understanding Work Ethics and Integrity
    • Professional Discipline and Accountability
    • Teamwork, Respect, and Organizational Culture
    • Building a Lasting Professional Image
    • Review and Wrap-Up
Learning Outcomes

By the end of this training, participants will be able to:

  • Understand the key traits of a strong and professional personality.
  • Identify their personal strengths and areas for improvement.
  • Demonstrate positive attitude, confidence, and emotional balance at work.
  • Apply ethical principles in decision-making and daily behavior.
  • Understand the impact of integrity, punctuality, and accountability.
  • Develop interpersonal skills for better teamwork and communication.
  • Build habits that reflect professionalism and reliability.
Course Methodology

The training uses a blended approach of self-assessments, interactive lectures, group discussions, role plays, and reflective exercises. Emphasis is placed on behavioral awareness and practical workplace application to ensure sustained change.

Activities
  • Personality Self-Assessment: Identify your personality strengths and blind spots.
  • Positive Attitude Exercise: Convert workplace complaints into constructive thoughts.
  • Role-Playing: Practice empathy, teamwork, and professionalism.
  • Ethical Dilemma Discussion: Analyze real-world ethical challenges.
  • Code of Conduct Drafting: Define personal and organizational behavioral standards.
  • Reflection Journal: Plan short-term actions for continuous self-improvement.
