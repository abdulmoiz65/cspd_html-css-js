Program Overview:

Procurement is called “source-to-settle” or “procure to pay” process. The process involves a careful evaluation, selection, and creation of formal documents as well as managing the company's ongoing supplier relationships. Procurement is a complex discipline spanning many interrelated activities of a supply chain.
Procurement supports operational requirements, manages supplier base effectively & efficiently and to develop strong relationships with external and internal sources of the company. It eventually supports organizational goals and objectives.
The objective of this course is help understand the procurement management under the umbrella of Supply chain management. The course will cover the major aspects of procurement management including P2P cycle, planning, spend management, contract management & negotiation.

Topics Covered:
  • Unit 1: Supply Chain Management
  • Unit 2: Understanding Procurement
  • Unit 3: Procure to Pay Cycle
  • Unit 4: Strategic Procurement Planning
  • Unit 5: Spend Management & Commodity Strategy
  • Unit 6: Buyer – Supplier Relationship
  • Unit 7: Contract Management
  • Unit 8: Negotiation Management

  • All participants will be required to provide their insights and inputs on all topics, where needed

Will be able to:

  • Comprehend Supply chain Strategy and its effect on Procurement policy of the any given organization.
  • Apply tools & techniques to procurement inventories. For E.g. ABC Analysis, Spend Analysis, Commodity Strategy

  • Understand & apply negotiation techniques to arrive win-win situation for both parties (Buyer & supplier)

Cancellation & Deferral Policy

    In the event of a participant’s cancellation, the following policy will apply:

  • 5 days before start: No cancellation fee.
  • Within 4–2 days: 50% forfeited.
  • Within 1 day or no-shows: 100% forfeited.

All cancellation requests must be submitted in writing to the Center for Skills and Professional Development (CSPD).

In case of any unforeseen circumstances, CSPD – MAJU reserves the right to postpone the workshop. In such cases, participants may either:

• Request a full refund,

• Defer their registration to the next available session of the same program.

Organizations may nominate a substitute participant up to 2 days before the workshop start date. In case an application cannot be accommodated, the deposited payment will be returned.

All deferral requests must be made in writing at least 2 days before the workshop.

Participants may defer their registration to another session of the same program within six months. The deposited fee will be carried forward.

Please note that only one deferral is permitted. After six months, the deposited fee will be forfeited.
